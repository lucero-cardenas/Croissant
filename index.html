Croissant

A croissant (UK: /ˈkrwʌsɒŋ/; , US: /krəsɒnt/; French pronunciation: [kʁwa.sɑ̃]) is a buttery, flaky, viennoiserie pastry of Austrian origin, but mostly associated with France. Croissants are named for its historical crescent shape and, like other viennoiserie, are made of a layered yeast-leavened dough.
Fun fact: In Mexico they're called "cuernitos" ((meaning "little horns") Mexican pronunciation: [/kwər.nee.toss/]).
